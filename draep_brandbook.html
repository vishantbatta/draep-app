03 — Visual Identity

Warm Precision

The exactness of an engineering tool, the warmth of an Indian home. The measuring tape is the source of every shape, pattern, and motion.

Color Physics

Deep Ink Navy carries trust and calm; a warm Draep Orange tape gradient brings energy and celebration. Together they are the brand handshake — never one without the other. Orange is the spice, not the base.

08 — Logo Usage

Using the Mark

The measuring tape looped into an alpha — treat it like a finished garment: clean edges, room to breathe, worn on the right backgrounds.

On light — preferred
Reversed on navy

Clear space & size

Keep a margin equal to the height of the wordmark's “a” on every side. Never render the symbol below 24px on screen (8mm print) or the primary lockup below 96px wide.

Backgrounds

Full-color logo on Warm Sand, Chalk White, or Mist Navy. Reversed logo on Ink Navy or a navy photo overlay of 40%+. Never on busy or light images.

File formats

SVG for web & app, transparent PNG at 2×/3× for raster, PDF/EPS for print & embroidery, and single-color navy/white for stamps and one-color work.

✅ Do

  • Keep the full-color mark on sand, white, or mist navy
  • Use the reversed lockup on navy or dark photos
  • Respect the clear-space margin on every side
  • Lead with the symbol alone for icons & avatars
  • Scale proportionally, always from a corner

❌ Don't

  • Recolor the tape or flatten its gradient
  • Set the wordmark in another typeface or uppercase
  • Rotate, outline, add shadows to, or stretch the lockup
  • Place the full-color logo on orange or busy photos
  • Separate the ticks from the tape
09 — Merchandise

Physical & Merch Design

Draep in the real world — uniforms, packaging, and the tape itself. Tailored, not printed-on: embroidery and woven labels over loud graphics.

Tailored, not printed-on

Favor embroidery, woven labels, debossing, and tonal prints. The brand is craft, not merch.

Navy + sand base

Keep garments and packaging in Ink Navy, Warm Sand, or natural cotton; use Draep Orange only as thread — stitching, tape trim, a single tab.

The tape is the signature

A measuring-tape trim, tick marks, or the alpha loop reads as Draep instantly — use it as edging, seams, and zip pulls.

Apparel
  • Style Captain polo — navy body, orange symbol on the chest, woven wordmark at the back collar
  • T-shirt — sand or white, tonal tape-loop print, one orange line: “Measured for you”
  • Apron — navy canvas, orange tape trim, tick-divided pockets
  • Cap — symbol front-center, wordmark on the back strap
  • Tote / garment bag — natural cotton, navy wordmark, one orange handle band
Packaging & collateral
  • Garment box — sand board, navy wordmark, orange tape “seal”, tick-pattern interior
  • Woven label — navy label, orange symbol, order ID in Plex Mono
  • Business card — navy stock, reversed logo, orange-painted edge
  • Branded tape — sand tape, navy numerals, orange rivet end, wordmark at 0

✅ Do

  • Embroider or deboss the mark
  • Keep to one accent color
  • Respect clear space on fabric
  • Use durable, natural materials

❌ Don't

  • Stretch the logo across a whole chest
  • Use neon or off-palette colors
  • Combine multiple large marks
  • Print the gradient on textured fabric — use solid orange thread
